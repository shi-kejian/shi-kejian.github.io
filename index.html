Bio

I am an M.S. student in Computer Science at Yale University, advised by Arman Cohan at the YaleNLP group. Previously, I completed my undergraduate studies at NYU, where I had the pleasure of collaborating with Sam Bowman's ARG group (a collaboration that continues today). My research centers around pretraining, evaluation, and AI safety.

My research focuses on developing safety-critical, robust and reliable frameworks for evaluation. I am driven by fundamental questions in AI assessment: How can we reliably compare language models' generative capabilities while ensuring robust evaluation? How do we verify that performance metrics translate to application safety, particularly when societal implications are at stake? How can we evaluate emerging LLM capabilities when ground truth may be uncertain?

Recently, I have been working on improving instruction-following evaluation and scalable oversight evaluation. On a personal development note, I am working on maintaining focused attention and managing dopamine responses - a continuous journey of growth.
